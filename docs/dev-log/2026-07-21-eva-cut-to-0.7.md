# Decision record — EVA is cut from 0.6 and moves to 0.7

**Date:** 2026-07-21 · **Authority:** Shinichi Nakagawa (maintainer), in session ·
**Recorded by:** Claude Code · **Supersedes:** the 2026-07-20 decision to include EVA in 0.6

---

## 1. The decision (maintainer, verbatim)

- gllvmTMB **0.6 ships Laplace-only**. EVA moves to **0.7**.
- 0.7's EVA should target **sparse binary first**, not the `q = 1` multi-trial cell.
- Failures an arc did not cause may **not** be waived on the agent's judgment; each
  needs individual maintainer sign-off and a durable record.

## 2. Reasoning — `AGENT-INFERRED`, not the maintainer's stated reasons

Recorded separately so a later reader never mistakes agent rationale for maintainer
intent. Reasons 1 and 2 are the load-bearing ones; an adversarial review panel found
the decision survives on those two alone.

1. **The cost estimate moved four- to nine-fold.** The 2026-07-20 decision rested on a
   ~30–45 hour estimate. `LOOP/arcs.md` estimates **4–7 weeks** for the minimal public
   version, against a ~2026-08-03 CRAN target. Laplace-only is ~6–10 working days.

2. **Design 85 is unproven with a known numerical weakness — NOT "the estimator failed."**
   The NO-GO (2026-07-20) stands because its predeclared **Gate-3 experiment was never
   obtained**: the pilot runner conflated the fixed-rank Gate 3 with the ML-rank Gate 4,
   and 8 of the applicable q1/q2 fits failed the predeclared optimiser gate. Gate 4 was
   invalidated by that conflation; Gate 5 (`q = 4/6` stress) never ran.
   *An earlier draft of this record said "Design 85 failed at the easy end." That was a
   mischaracterisation and is corrected here.*

3. **Laplace is the documented DEFAULT engine.** `docs/design/04-sister-package-scope.md`
   excludes *"variational approximation **as the primary inference engine**"* — gllvmTMB
   uses Laplace, `gllvm` is the VA alternative. Note the precise scope: it excludes VA as
   the *primary* engine; it never excluded an optional non-default `method =`.

4. **The sketched narrow cell was a research-only verification gate** — no 0.6 user-facing
   capability under any outcome, so cuttable at zero user cost, while remaining the correct
   FIRST step whenever EVA revives.
   *Correction a later reader must not lose: **Design 86 was never written or approved.**
   It exists only as a sketch inside `LOOP/ultra-plan.md` and `LOOP/arcs.md`, and
   `LOOP/decision-queue.md` records it as `NOT YET OPEN`. Do not cite it as an existing
   contract. "Balanced" was also not in the contract — Design 85 requires **complete**,
   and admits `n_it = 2`.*

## 3. The 0.7 target, and what it actually costs

### Name the estimators separately

These are different objects and must not both be called "EVA":

| Name | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Design 85 estimator** | full-covariance **Gaussian VA**, 1-D Gauss–Hermite expectation |
| **EVA** (Korhonen et al. 2023) | **second-order Taylor surrogate** |

Any 0.7 contract must state which one it targets.

### Separate the correctness anchor from the admission criterion

The sketched Design 86 conflated *proving the estimator is correct* with *proving it is
worth having*. They are different gates:

| | Purpose | Cell | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Correctness anchor** | is the estimator right? | easy, verifiable, information-rich | necessary, never sufficient — Design 72 sequencing still binds |
| **Admission criterion** | is it worth shipping? | **sparse binary, high latent dimension** | the actual gate; nothing ships that only works where Laplace already works |

**Consequence:** a "minimal EVA" is permanently not worth shipping. 0.7's EVA is the broad
version or it is nothing.

### Three cautions on the sparse-binary target

1. **`UNVERIFIED — source not supplied`:** the crisp `q >= 4` threshold comes from an
   uncited literature summary. It **must be citation-checked before it is used to define
   the 0.7 admission gate**, not merely before manuscript use. State the criterion in its
   defensible form — per-unit information per latent dimension (roughly
   `T * median n_it / q`) below a predeclared floor — rather than a bare integer cut-point.
2. **Sparse binary at high `q` lies OUTSIDE Design 85's admitted data contract** (Bernoulli
   excluded by analogy; projected-variance fail-closed at 4). It requires a **new Gate-0
   scope freeze and Gate-1 oracle receipt** — *not* a `q`-extension of existing evidence.
3. **The second-order surrogate's fixed-order bias is worst in exactly this regime.**

### Cost

`10–16+ weeks` prices the OLD non-sparse scope. **The sparse-binary target is UNESTIMATED.**

## 4. What must NOT be lost

- Design 85's NO-GO stands verbatim. **`docs/design/85-*` is READ-ONLY.** Never amend a
  closed NO-GO, never relabel its pilot, never tune its gates, never salvage selected
  successes, never run `q4`/`q6` against it. Supersede with a new dated note instead.
- Design 72's sequential proof logic: a later gate never compensates for an earlier failure.
- No EVA objective may be called an ELBO, marginal likelihood, REML objective, or
  likelihood-comparable quantity unless an approved mathematical contract establishes that
  exact claim.

## 5. Open questions this decision did not settle

- **Is EVA ever gllvmTMB's job?** `gllvm` already occupies the VA/EVA niche competently.
  Complementarity *across packages* is cheaper than duplication *within* one. This is a
  real 0.7 question, not a foregone conclusion.
- **Venue** — JSS vs Methods in Ecology and Evolution. Does not block 0.6. Note that JSS is
  a *software* journal: methodological novelty in the estimator is not a review criterion,
  and gllvmTMB's contribution is the stacked-trait grammar, not the integration method.
- **Documentation debt this creates.** `DESCRIPTION` advertises binary presence-absence
  JSDM — precisely the regime where Laplace is weakest — and no vignette currently caveats
  Laplace accuracy or points readers to `gllvm` for it. Deferred to M4.
