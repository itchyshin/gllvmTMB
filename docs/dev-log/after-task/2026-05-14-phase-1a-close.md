### Darwin (biology + visualization priorities)

> "Three sub-matrices, three biological questions. $\Sigma_B^{(u)}$ =
> personality syndrome (Sih et al. 2004); $\Sigma_B^{(b)}$ =
> plasticity syndrome (Dingemanse & Dochtermann 2013);
> $\Sigma_B^{(u,b)}$ = personality-plasticity association --
> **the most misread sub-matrix**: collapsing a tilted 2D
> distribution into 'bold = more plastic, r = 0.42' loses the
> rank-reversal information."

Five priority visualizations:

1. Personality-syndrome heatmap on $\text{cor}(\Sigma_B^{(u)})$
2. Plasticity-syndrome heatmap on $\text{cor}(\Sigma_B^{(b)})$
3. **Reaction-norm spaghetti plot** (per-individual lines,
   faceted by trait) -- *"Skipping it makes the framework feel
   matrix-algebraic rather than biological. It's the only
   graphic where the reader can hand-draw which two
   individuals 'swap rank' across the gradient -- a
   behavioural-ecology mainstay since Nussey et al. 2007."*
4. **Intercept-slope BLUP scatter with 95 % bivariate
   ellipse, per trait** -- the antidote to the canonical
   misreading.
5. $R_t(\text{temp})$ curve across the temperature range
   (Eq. 50).

Sex-specific syndromes (Appendix B.2): side-by-side
$\mathbf{C}_B^{(F)}$ and $\mathbf{C}_B^{(M)}$ heatmaps on
shared colour scale + a third **difference panel** with
diverging scale centred at 0.

Missing worked-example question: *"Does temperature
variability erode the boldness-activity syndrome?"* -- compute
$\Sigma_B(x)$ (Eq. 54) across the empirical gradient, plot
$\text{cor}(\Sigma_B(x))_{\text{bold,activity}}$ vs temp.
*"This is the question O'Dea et al. (2022) flagged but could
not answer with separate univariate fits."*

### Fisher (identifiability + recovery test design)

> "Reaction-norm leverage matters: $J_i \geq 3$ with at least
> 2 distinct temp values per individual is the floor.
> $N \geq 10 \cdot (2T d_B + 2T)$ rule of thumb for stable MLE.
> Sign indeterminacy in $\Lambda_B$ spans intercept + slope
> blocks jointly -- pin the canonical sign on the combined
> $(2T) \times d_B$ matrix, not block-by-block, or
> $\Sigma_B^{(u,b)}$ flips sign across refits."

Five recovery-test DGPs locked for
`tests/testthat/test-random-slope-recovery.R` (RS-1
aligned, RS-2 two-axis, RS-3 boundary $\text{Cov}(u, b) = 0$,
RS-4 degenerate $\text{Var}(b) = 0$, RS-5 mixed-attribute sex).

`check_identifiability()` extensions: three new flag classes
(`$flags$intercept_slope_decoupled`, `$flags$slope_boundary`,
`$flags$temp_within_var_low`).

**Centering trap no existing diagnostic catches**: Eq. 48
shows $\rho_{u,b,t}$ is contaminated when temp is not centred
at 0. Fit-time check: if `|mean(x) / sd(x)| > 0.1`, emit
`cli::cli_warn` referencing Eq. 47 + add
`$flags$slope_covariate_uncentred`.

## What did not go smoothly

- **The audit-doc Batch E framing was wrong on the
  `\mathbf{U}` interpretation.** The 2026-05-13
  post-overnight drift-scan audit listed Batch E as
  "`\mathbf{U}` → `\boldsymbol{\Psi}` in
  `behavioural-syndromes.Rmd`," but on inspection the
  `\mathbf{U}_B/W` references in that vignette are
  **individual-score matrices** (the LHS factor scores
  $\mathbf{Z}_B$ from Eq. 44), not the unique-variance
  matrix $\boldsymbol\Psi$. The correct replacement was
  `\mathbf{Z}_B/W`, matching the paper. Caught by reading
  the actual line context before editing. **Lesson**:
  audit-doc scope items that name a notation transformation
  should be verified against the actual line context, not
  the audit-doc framing, because notation symbols are
  re-used across roles in factor-analytic notation.
- **The 2026-05-12 + 2026-05-14 "two-U task label
  retention" decisions had to be overridden 2 days later.**
  Pre-CRAN scope is in flux; the right move is to record
  the override entry in `decisions.md` (done) and explain
  the rationale, not to amend the old entries (decisions.md
  is append-only). The retention decision was reasonable at
  the time given the math-vs-task-label distinction; the
  retirement decision is reasonable now that PIC-MOM is
  superseded by `check_identifiability()`. Both entries
  remain in canon to show the deliberation.
- **Branch-fork timing**: this PR's branch was forked from
  `agent/phase1a-close` at `7c99f34` (PR #94's squash

  disjoint files (PR #95: `morphometrics.Rmd` +
